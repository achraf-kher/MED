New Concept : Exterior Side Air Bags

Over the past three decades, we've gotten used to things blowing up in our faces. The advent of airbags has done wonders to protect cars' fragile flesh-and-blood cargo when vehicles go smash. Lots of people—especially those who've been protected from injury or death by an airbag deployment—must have wondered: What if we put the same technology on the outside of the car? One of those people is me..

Sensors

External side airbags are a type of supplemental restraint system designed to deploy from the wheelbase of a vehicle during a side impact collision. The deployment of external side airbags is typically triggered by sensors that detect a side-impact collision.

Deployment

Once deployed, the airbags are designed to cover the side under the windows and extend downward to protect the occupants'. Some systems may also include additional sensors to detect the impact's angle and adjust the airbags' deployment pattern accordingly.

Airbag Systems

The entire system works quickly to calculate if an external airbag is needed on one side or the other. Sensors, lidar, radar, and cameras work together to give the system a digital checklist before deploying an airbag. The system first checks to see if a crash is avoidable, and if it's not, special algorithms calculate if the external airbag will be beneficial and help protect the occupants. If the system gives a greenlight for an external side airbag to fire, the inflators fire up and fill the airbag just as the impact occurs. it all happens in 150 milliseconds—about the amount of time it takes for a human to blink his or her eyes. The bag fills from the bottom up with 280 to 400 liters of air, which is five to eight times that of a standard steering wheel airbag. The bag fills the area between the A- and C-pillars.
